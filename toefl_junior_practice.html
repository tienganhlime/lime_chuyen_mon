🎯 What is Part 3?
You hear a short conversation — between students, or a teacher and students — about everyday school life. Then you are asked to retell the conversation to a friend who was absent.
⏱️ 45 seconds to prepare → 1 minute to speak
📝 Note-taking: use a blank sheet of paper

In the real test you only get blank paper. The topic changes every time — so learn to make your own notes, not fill in a fixed table. Here's how:

👇 Write like this — key words only, not full sentences

Who's talking? → e.g. Teacher + 2 students
Main news / task: → e.g. field trip – Nat. Museum – Fri
Details: → e.g. bring: notebook + pencils / sketch 3 exhibits
Q&A: → e.g. Q: pairs? → No, individual
⭐ Key action for absent friend: → e.g. leave 8:30, wear comfy shoes, due Mon
💡 Tip: Write words + arrows. Example: paper → torn, not crumpled → dry. You're making a map, not a transcript!
🗣️ Speaking template
"Hey [name], you missed class — let me fill you in."
"The teacher told us that MAIN TASK."
"She also explained that HOW / DETAILS."
"[A student] asked about QUESTION, and the teacher said ANSWER."
"It's due DEADLINE, so make sure you ACTION."
"Oh, and one more thing — EXTRA DETAIL."
She also said… On top of that… The reason is… So that means… One student asked… Oh, and…
🏆 Score 4 vs Score 2
❌ Score 2
"Teacher say homework. Chapter six. Ten question. Wednesday."
✅ Score 4
"The teacher told us to read Chapter 6 and answer ten questions in full sentences. It's due Wednesday."
The difference: Score 4 uses full sentences and connector words. Score 2 just lists words.

🎯 What is Part 4?
You hear a teacher explain an academic topic (science, nature, history…). While listening, you see pictures or a video on screen to help you understand. Then you are asked to summarize the talk.
⏱️ 45 seconds to prepare → 1 minute to speak
📝 Note-taking on blank paper
👇 Sketch this shape — adapt it to whatever you hear

TOPIC: ___________________________

Point 1: _____ → detail: _____
Point 2: _____ → detail: _____
Point 3: _____ → detail: _____ (if there is one)

⭐ Conclusion / why it matters: _______
💡 Look at the picture while listening — it gives you the vocabulary! If you see a frog life cycle diagram, the words you need are right there.
🗣️ Speaking template
"In this talk, the teacher explained TOPIC."
"First, she said that POINT 1."
"She also pointed out that POINT 2."
"In addition, POINT 3." (if you have one)
"Overall, CONCLUSION / WHY IT MATTERS."
First… She also explained… This means that… As a result… For example… Overall…
