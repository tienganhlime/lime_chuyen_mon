🎯 What is Task 3 (Opinion)?
You read a topic/question, then write 1 paragraph (100–150 words) expressing your opinion with reasons and examples. You have 10 minutes.
⚠️ Important — this is NOT an essay! Unlike FCE where you write 4–5 paragraphs, Task 3 is just ONE paragraph. Your introduction and conclusion are single sentences inside that paragraph.
📊 How it's scored — Official ETS Rubric (0–4)
🌟 Score 4 — Target!
Clear position + specific supporting details/examples · Well organized & coherent · Good vocabulary variety · Varied sentence structure · Minor errors only (don't affect meaning)
✅ Score 3
Clear position + support (but may be incomplete) · Generally organized, occasional clarity lapses · Some vocabulary & sentence variety · Some errors (occasionally affect meaning)
📈 Score 2
Position stated but support is inadequate OR position only implied · Connections unclear/missing · Little vocabulary variety, simple/repetitive sentences · Errors frequently affect meaning
📚 Score 1
No clear position OR incoherent support · Unorganized · Very limited vocabulary used incorrectly · Mostly incorrect sentence structures · Many errors seriously affect meaning
💡 The 2 key things that separate Score 3 from Score 4: (1) Specific examples — not general claims, but real/named situations; (2) Lexical variation — don't repeat the same words, show you know synonyms and connectors.
🏗️ The 7-sentence template — use this every time
Sentence 1 — Opinion (choose a side):
"I think/believe that restate topic + your position."

Sentence 2 — Reason 1:
"The first reason is that reason 1."

Sentence 3 — Example for Reason 1:
"For example, concrete example from school/daily life."

Sentence 4 — Reason 2:
"Another reason is that reason 2."

Sentence 5 — Example for Reason 2:
"For instance, concrete example."

Sentence 6 — Counter + Dismiss (optional but scores higher):
"Some people think that opposite view, but why your side is still correct."

Sentence 7 — Conclusion:
"For these reasons, I believe that restate opinion in different words."
⚡ Key differences from FCE essay
FCE: 4–5 paragraphs, balanced discussion, 140–190 words, formal
Task 3: 1 paragraph only, clearly take 1 side, 100–150 words, semi-formal

👉 Don't do: write separate paragraphs, write "On the one hand / on the other hand" as a balanced argument, exceed 150 words.
👉 Do: state your side clearly in sentence 1, give 2 focused reasons with real examples.
🎯 Strategy for picking a side
Pick the side where you can think of 2 specific examples right away — not the side you actually agree with. You have 10 minutes, so choose whatever's easier to write fast.
✅ Connector words to use
The first reason is… Another reason is… For example, For instance, In addition, However, Some people think… but… For these reasons,
❌ vs ✅ — What good development looks like
❌ Too vague (loses points)
"Mobile phones are useful for students because they can look things up."
✅ Specific example (scores high)
"For example, during self-study a student can look up the meaning of an unfamiliar word without interrupting the teacher."

🎯 What is Task 4 (Listen-Write)?
You listen to a teacher's talk (social studies, science, geography…) once only. While listening you take notes. Then you write a paragraph explaining what you heard. You have 10 minutes.
⚠️ Key challenge: The question appears after you listen — but it almost always asks for reasons / causes / how something works. So take notes on the WHY and HOW while listening.
📊 How it's scored — Official ETS Rubric (0–4)
🌟 Score 4 — Target!
Accurately provides ALL key points · Uses relevant supporting details from the talk · Well organized & coherent · Shows lexical variation · Varied sentence structure · Minor errors only (don't affect meaning)
✅ Score 3
Accurately provides MOST key points · Some supporting details from the talk · Generally organized, occasional clarity lapses · Some lexical & sentence variety · Some errors (occasionally affect meaning)
📈 Score 2
Only SOME accurate content · Minimal or no supporting details · Connections between ideas unclear/missing · Little vocabulary variety, simple sentences · Errors frequently affect meaning
📚 Score 1
Minimal or NO content from key points · No details beyond the visuals · Incoherent · Extremely limited vocabulary · Mostly incorrect sentence structures · Many errors seriously affect meaning
💡 The 2 key things that separate Score 3 from Score 4: (1) ALL key points captured — not just 2 out of 3; (2) Specific details & numbers — don't just name a point, include the exact figure or fact the teacher mentioned.
📝 Note-taking template — draw this on paper BEFORE audio starts
TOPIC: _________________________________

WHY / HOW 1: _____________________________
   → detail / number: _______________________

WHY / HOW 2: _____________________________
   → detail / number: _______________________

WHY / HOW 3 (if mentioned): _______________
   → detail / number: _______________________

RESULT / SO WHAT: _____________________
🔑 5 note-taking rules
1
⭐ MOST IMPORTANT: Listen with 100% focus to the FIRST 2 sentences The teacher ALWAYS states the topic + structure in the opening. This is your whole "map" — if you miss it, you're lost for the entire talk.

🎧 Example — you hear: "Today we are going to talk about why the rainforest receives so much rain. There are three main reasons for this."
✅ Note: TOPIC = rainforest rain → 3 reasons

🎧 Another example: "In today's lesson, I'll explain how the human heart pumps blood around the body."
✅ Note: TOPIC = heart → how pumps blood

Now you know exactly what to listen for: 3 reasons (or "how it works"). Every sentence after becomes easier to organise.
2
Keywords only — never full sentences Your hand cannot keep up with speech. Drop grammar words entirely — only keep content words.

❌ "The eastern side of the island gets a lot of rain because of the trade winds"
✅ "East side → lots rain ← trade winds"

❌ "Monarch butterflies travel approximately 4,500 kilometres every year"
✅ "Monarch butterfly → 4,500 km/yr"
3
Numbers & proper nouns — write IMMEDIATELY, even if you miss what comes next Numbers and names cannot be guessed. The moment you hear one, stop everything and write it.

🎧 "…with temperatures reaching minus 60 degrees Celsius…" → write −60°C NOW
🎧 "…called the Amazon Basin, which covers 7 million square kilometres…" → write Amazon = 7M km² NOW
🎧 "…a process known as photosynthesis, which produces glucose…" → write photosynthesis → glucose NOW

Missing one number in your notes = losing a whole point on the "Details" criterion.
4
Use symbols & abbreviations — build your own system The more automatic your symbols, the faster you write. Suggested shortcuts:

→ = causes / leads to  ·  ← = because of  ·  ↑ = increase  ·  ↓ = decrease
bc = because  ·  eg = for example  ·  ∴ = therefore  ·  ≈ = approximately
w/ = with  ·  w/o = without  ·  info = information  ·  env = environment

Example talk sentence: "As a result, polar bears have developed thick fur to survive."
✅ Note: "∴ polar bear → thick fur → survive"
5
Missed something? Write "?" and keep going immediately Stopping to think costs you the NEXT sentence too. The moment you fall behind, write "?" and move on.

❌ Wrong: you heard "…absorbs 97% of…" but missed what — you pause to guess → miss point 3 entirely
✅ Right: write "absorbs 97% ?" and immediately listen for the next point

A note that says "WHY 2 → ocean absorbs 97% ?" is still useful when writing. A blank is not.
🗒️ See it in action — full note example (Coral Reefs)
You HEAR: "Today we're going to discuss how coral reefs are formed. There are mainly two stages."
You WRITE: TOPIC = coral reefs → how formed → 2 stages

You HEAR: "In the first stage, tiny animals called polyps attach to hard surfaces on the ocean floor. Each polyp is only about one millimetre wide."
You WRITE: Stage 1: polyps → attach to hard surface · size = 1mm

You HEAR: "Over hundreds of years, millions of polyps build up layers of calcium carbonate — the same material as chalk — creating the reef structure."
You WRITE: Stage 2: millions polyps → calcium carbonate (=chalk) → reef · 100s yrs

RESULT: 3 details you can use in writing: polyps, 1mm, calcium carbonate/chalk
🗒️ Full note example 2 — Monarch Butterfly Migration
You HEAR: "Today I want to explain why monarch butterflies migrate every year. There are three key reasons they make this long journey."
You WRITE: TOPIC = monarch butterfly → WHY migrate → 3 reasons ← write this IMMEDIATELY

You HEAR: "The first reason is temperature. Monarch butterflies cannot survive temperatures below 0 degrees Celsius. In winter, their home in Canada becomes too cold."
You WRITE: R1: temp · <0°C → Canada too cold winter

You HEAR: "The second reason is food supply. Milkweed plants — the only thing monarchs eat — die off in northern areas during autumn."
You WRITE: R2: food · milkweed (only food) → dies autumn north

You HEAR: "Finally, monarch butterflies travel approximately 4,500 kilometres to reach their winter home in the mountains of central Mexico, where they gather in groups of millions."
You WRITE: R3: dest = Mexico mountains · 4,500km · millions gather

RESULT: ✅ 4 specific details captured: 0°C, milkweed, 4,500km, Mexico mountains
🗒️ Full note example 3 — The Water Cycle
You HEAR: "In today's class we'll look at how the water cycle works. It has four main stages."
You WRITE: TOPIC = water cycle → how works → 4 stages

You HEAR: "Stage one is evaporation. Heat from the sun causes water from oceans, lakes, and rivers to turn into water vapour and rise into the atmosphere."
You WRITE: S1: evaporation · sun heat → water → vapour → rise

You HEAR: "In stage two, called condensation, the vapour cools as it rises and forms clouds made of tiny water droplets."
You WRITE: S2: condensation · vapour cools → clouds (tiny droplets)

You HEAR: "Stage three is precipitation — when droplets become too heavy, they fall as rain or snow."
You WRITE: S3: precipitation · too heavy → rain/snow

You HEAR: "Finally, in stage four, water collects in rivers and flows back to the ocean, completing the cycle. This process takes between days and thousands of years depending on where the water ends up."
You WRITE: S4: collection · rivers → ocean · cycle complete · time = days–1000s yrs

RESULT: ✅ Perfect: all 4 stages + key vocabulary (evaporation, condensation, precipitation) captured
❌ vs ✅ — Bad notes vs Good notes (same audio)
❌ Bad notes — too slow, too long
Audio: "Monarch butterflies travel approximately 4,500 kilometres to central Mexico."

Student writes: "The butterflies travel a very long distance to the south every year in winter."

→ ❌ No number (4,500 km) · No name (Mexico) · Written like an essay sentence · Hand couldn't keep up with audio
✅ Good notes — fast, specific
Audio: "Monarch butterflies travel approximately 4,500 kilometres to central Mexico."

Student writes: "→ Mexico · ≈4,500km"

→ ✅ Key number saved · Destination saved · Written in 2 seconds · Can rebuild the full sentence during writing time
🚦 Teacher transition signals — these tell you a new point is coming!
Train your ear to catch these phrases: When you hear them, start a new line in your notes immediately.

📢 "The first reason is…" → R1:
📢 "Another reason / The second reason…" → R2:
📢 "In addition / Furthermore / Also…" → R3:
📢 "For example / For instance…" → eg: (write the example detail!)
📢 "This means that / As a result…" → ∴ (consequence/result)
📢 "Now let's look at… / Moving on…" → new section starting
📢 "Finally / In conclusion…" → last point — don't miss it!

💡 Pro tip: In TOEFL Junior, the teacher ALWAYS uses clear transition language. These phrases are free gifts — they tell you exactly when to start writing a new line.
🏗️ The 7–9 sentence writing template
Sentence 1 — Topic sentence:
"TOPIC is/are brief description of what the talk explained."

Sentences 2–3 — Point 1 (main reason/point + detail):
"The first reason is that Point 1. For example/This means that detail or number."

Sentences 4–5 — Point 2:
"On the other hand / Another reason is that Point 2. detail or number."

Sentences 6–7 — Point 3 (if you have it):
"In addition, Point 3. detail."

Sentences 8–9 — Conclusion:
"In conclusion, the differences/process/causes are mainly due to root cause, which result."
✅ Connector words for Task 4
The first reason is… This means that… On the other hand, Another reason is… For example, As a result, In addition, In conclusion,
